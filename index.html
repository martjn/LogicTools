Alphabets

An alphabet is a standardized set of basic written graphemes (called letters) that represent the phonemes of certain spoken languages. Not all writing systems represent language in this way; in a syllabary, each character represents a syllable, and logographic systems use characters to represent words, morphemes, or other semantic units.

Classic Ciphers

In the realm of cryptography, a cipher is a technique used to secure data by encoding and decoding it. While most ciphers necessitate a distinct key for both processes, some, such as ROT13 or Atbash, use predetermined keys. Historically, many of these ciphers were crucial for military or other important purposes, but nowadays, they are primarily employed by puzzle enthusiasts.
